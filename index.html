Abstract

Physics-based simulation is crucial for robotic arm control and reinforcement learning, yet bridging the Real2Sim and Sim2Real gaps remains challenging due to the complex properties of robots and manipulated objects. Our work addresses these challenges by introducing a hybrid representation model that integrates mesh geometry, Gaussian color, and physics attributes for improved robotic arm Digital Asset representation. This model facilitates accurate reconstruction of high-fidelity digital assets from monocular videos, supporting both static and dynamic rendering through Gaussian Splatting techniques.

We propose a novel system that encompasses the entire robotic manipulation process from Real2Sim to Sim2Real. The Real2Sim component reconstructs manipulable textured meshes and labels individual robot parts and objects with their physics attributes. Our method includes real-world motion in digital assets, ensuring accurate representation of mass and friction, crucial for robotic manipulation. This hybrid representation is bound through a Gaussian-Mesh-Pixel binding technique, establishing an isomorphic mapping between mesh vertices and Gaussian models to maintain consistent motion and optimize temporal variations.

For the Sim2Real transition, our engine rendering the trained policy with high fidelity, applying it to real-world robotic tasks. Our approach includes a comprehensive simulation pipeline that standardizes coordinate systems and scales, ensuring consistent integration of multiple components. Pre-trained policies are tested in the simulation environment and validated through real-world deployment, achieving seamless transfer of grasping policies.

We provide datasets of robotic manipulation and several benchmarks for mesh reconstruction, as well as 3D and 4D rendering. Our model achieves state-of-the-art results in 4D rendering and mesh reconstruction quality for robotic applications.



Our system consists of four modules to achieve the functionalities: The Production and Extraction Backend processes video data to generate accurate and textured 3D models of robotic arms using Gaussian splatting techniques. The Physics Engine Backend integrates with various physics engines to simulate realistic interactions and behaviors of robotic arms. The Splatting Backend enables detailed scenes reconstruction and rendering for both static and dynamic scenarios. The Task Backend facilitates a wide range of robotic tasks such as manipulation and grasping, ensuring consistency across different coordinate systems. The modular nature of the pipeline allows for compatibility with various physics engines and robotic tasks, making it a versatile tool for robotic research and applications.
